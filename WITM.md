# WhatsintheMirror.org
## Website Audit & ROI-Focused Redesign

Prepared for WhatsintheMirror (WITM)

Healing justice, mental health awareness, and HIV stigma reduction for communities of color.

---

# Why This Audit

- WITM is a healing justice 501(c)(3) using art, advocacy, and affirming care to serve Black and Brown communities.
- The current site communicates the mission but does not yet feel like a comprehensive mental wellness database.
- This deck frames a redesign in terms of visceral value for visitors and ROI for WITM and partners.

---

# Who WITM Serves

- People of color living with HIV and/or navigating mental health challenges.
- Women, youth, and LGBTQIA+ communities seeking culturally affirming support.
- Artists, advocates, and mental wellness contributors of color who co-create healing spaces.

> Goal: A site that feels safe, affirming, and easy to navigate within seconds of landing.

---

4. Technical Health & Accessibility (high-level)

# UX & IA – Current State

- Homepage repeats the "Austin Texas Comprehensive Mental Wellness/Health Database" message.
- Limited clarity on next steps for new visitors (seek support, attend events, donate).
- Navigation is functional but does not foreground high-intent actions.
- No real directory interface, search, or filtering despite the "database" promise.

_Visual idea: show a screenshot of the current homepage with 3–4 callout annotations._

---

# UX & IA – Opportunities

- Make the homepage a clear story path instead of a generic landing page.
- Introduce explicit paths:
  - "Get Support" for people seeking mental health or HIV-related resources.
  - "Get Involved" for donors, volunteers, partners, and artists.
- Add a simple, phased directory experience that can grow over time.

_Visual idea: before/after wireframes of the hero, nav, and directory teaser._

---

# Proposed Homepage Structure

1. Hero: one-line value proposition + key CTAs.
2. "How WITM Helps" overview (3–4 cards: Art, Advocacy, Care, Community).
3. Featured Programs: Art Heals Festival, Bedside, other core initiatives.
4. Resource Highlights: mental health and HIV support resources.
5. Directory Teaser: "Find a mental wellness contributor of color in Austin".
6. Stories & Testimonials.
7. Donate / Get Involved strip.

---

# Content & Storytelling – Current State

- WITM has a powerful story rooted in lived experience and creative healing.
- Mission, vision, and impact live across the About page, program pages, and external articles.
- The homepage messaging is more functional than emotional.
- Impact stories and quotes are not consistently surfaced near key CTAs.

_Visual idea: collage of program photos with a short, bold quote overlay._

---

# Content & Storytelling – Recommendations

- Rewrite the hero to lead with a human-centered statement:
  > "Healing justice for Black and Brown communities living with mental health challenges and HIV."
- Add a concise "Why we exist" story section tying mission, vision, and values together.
- Use short, scannable impact proof:
  - Number of events and workshops hosted.
  - Artists and mental wellness contributors engaged.
  - Community members reached.
- Pair CTAs with quotes or brief testimonials.

---

# Visual Design – Current State

- Large headings and simple imagery, but no cohesive visual system.
- Color usage doesn’t fully reflect the energy of WITM’s art and programs.
- Typography and components feel more template-driven than brand-specific.

_Visual idea: show current site next to event artwork or photos to highlight the gap._

---

# Visual Design – Direction

- Color: grounded base colors (e.g., deep purple/indigo) with vibrant accents drawn from Art Heals and other programs.
- Type: modern sans-serif for body copy plus a bold display face for headlines.
- Layout: card-based sections for programs, events, and resources.
- Imagery: real WITM spaces, artists, and community members wherever possible.

_Design note: codify this into tokens (colors, typography, spacing) for implementation._

---

# Technical Health & Accessibility

High-level opportunities:

- Improve heading hierarchy and semantic markup.
- Add descriptive alt text to all key imagery.
- Compress and optimize images for faster load, especially on mobile.
- Introduce basic schema markup for:
  - Organization / Nonprofit
  - Events (Art Heals, Bedside)
  - Articles / Resources

_Visual idea: simple checklist or icon grid for Performance, SEO, Accessibility, AI-readiness._

---

# Conversion, Trust & ROI – Current State

- Donation and engagement CTAs exist but are easy to miss.
- Impact proof (stories, numbers, partners) is not consistently paired with asks.
- Paths from homepage → events/resources → donate are not designed as funnels.

_Visual idea: diagram showing where visitors drop off in the current flow._

---

# Conversion, Trust & ROI – Opportunities

- Make two primary actions unavoidable:
  - "Find Support" (resources, directory, contact).
  - "Support the Movement" (donate, volunteer, partner).
- Surround CTAs with impact proof:
  - Short stats.
  - Quotes from participants and collaborators.
  - Partner logos when appropriate.
- Clarify donation options with simple tiers tied to tangible outcomes.

_Visual idea: funnel graphic – Learn → Engage → Act – labeled with pages and KPIs._

---

# Phased Roadmap

**Phase 1 – Homepage & Navigation**

- New hero, streamlined navigation, explicit "Get Support / Get Involved" structure.
- Light visual refresh (color, type, card patterns).

**Phase 2 – Directory MVP**

- Simple, filterable list of mental wellness contributors of color in Austin.
- Admin-friendly way to add/update entries.

---

# Phased Roadmap (cont.)

**Phase 3 – Story-Driven Program Pages**

- Rebuild key program and event pages as evergreen story hubs.
- Add videos, photos, quotes, and clear next steps.

**Phase 4 – Technical, SEO & Accessibility**

- Implement performance, SEO, and accessibility improvements.
- Add structured data to improve search and AI discoverability.

_Visual idea: timeline or staircase graphic with 4 labeled steps._

---

# Measuring ROI

Potential KPIs by phase:

- Homepage & Nav
  - Bounce rate
  - Click-through to resources and donation page

- Directory MVP
  - Directory page visits
  - Profile views / contact clicks

- Story-Driven Pages
  - Event page views
  - Event registrations / interest form submissions

- Technical & Accessibility
  - Search impressions and clicks
  - Accessibility audit scores
